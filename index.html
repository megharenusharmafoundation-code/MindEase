Personalized Wellness Recommendations

Science-backed tips to improve your health and wellbeing.

🧠 Stress Reduction Tips

  • 1. Practice Deep Breathing: Spend 5-10 minutes daily doing deep breathing exercises. Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4.
  • 2. Meditation & Mindfulness: Start with just 5 minutes of meditation daily using apps like Calm or Headspace.
  • 3. Physical Activity: Exercise releases endorphins. Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity per week.
  • 4. Social Connections: Spend quality time with friends and family. Social support reduces stress significantly.
  • 5. Digital Detox: Reduce screen time, especially before bed. Aim for screen-free hours in the evening.

😴 Sleep Improvement Tips

  • 1. Consistent Schedule: Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily, even on weekends.
  • 2. Sleep Environment: Keep your bedroom cool (65-68°F), dark, and quiet.
  • 3. No Screens Before Bed: Avoid phones, tablets, and TVs for at least 30 minutes before sleep.
  • 4. Limit Caffeine & Alcohol: Avoid caffeine after 2 PM and alcohol before bed.
  • 5. Relaxation Routine: Try reading, gentle yoga, or journaling before bed.

🍎 Diet & Sugar Reduction Advice

  • 1. Whole Foods First: Base your diet on whole grains, lean proteins, fruits, and vegetables.
  • 2. Reduce Added Sugars: Limit sugary drinks, candy, and processed foods.
  • 3. Smart Swaps: Replace sugary drinks with water, herbal tea, or sparkling water.
  • 4. Healthy Snacks: Keep nuts, fruits, and yogurt handy for healthy snacking.
  • 5. Read Labels: Check food labels for hidden sugars and sodium content.

❤️ BP & Diabetes Prevention Tips

  • 1. DASH Diet: Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  • 2. Reduce Sodium: Aim for less than 2,300 mg of sodium per day.
  • 3. Regular Exercise: 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly helps control BP and blood sugar.
  • 4. Weight Management: Even 5-10% weight loss can significantly improve health markers.
  • 5. Regular Monitoring: Check your blood pressure and blood sugar levels regularly as recommended by your doctor.
